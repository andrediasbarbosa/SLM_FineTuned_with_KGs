# Knowledge Graphs

A knowledge graph is a structured representation of entities and their relationships.
Knowledge graphs provide a way to encode factual knowledge in a machine-readable format.
They are widely used in search engines, question answering systems, and recommendation engines.

Knowledge graphs support factual grounding and reasoning in AI systems.
By encoding domain knowledge as a graph, AI systems can leverage structured information
to improve accuracy and reduce hallucinations.

Entities in a knowledge graph are assigned types such as Person, Organization, or Concept.
Relations between entities are typed and directed, forming a labeled directed graph.
A hypergraph generalizes this by allowing a single relation to connect multiple head and tail entities.
