# Neural Networks

A neural network is a computational model inspired by the structure of biological brains.
Neural networks consist of layers of interconnected nodes that process input data through
learned weights and non-linear activation functions.

Deep neural networks have demonstrated strong performance on image recognition, natural
language processing, and game playing. However, they often lack interpretability and may
produce outputs that are inconsistent with known facts.

Language models are a class of neural networks trained to predict the next token in a
sequence. Large language models have shown impressive generalization but remain prone to
hallucination — generating plausible-sounding statements not supported by evidence.

Small language models aim to reduce computational cost while retaining sufficient capacity
for domain-specific reasoning tasks.
