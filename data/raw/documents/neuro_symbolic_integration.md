# Neuro-Symbolic Integration

Neuro-symbolic AI combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning to leverage the strengths
of both paradigms. Neural components provide pattern recognition and generalization, while
symbolic components provide interpretability, constraint satisfaction, and logical inference.

Knowledge graphs serve as a natural interface between neural and symbolic systems.
By grounding neural model outputs in validated knowledge graph facts, neuro-symbolic systems
can reduce hallucinations and improve factual consistency.

Graph-grounded fine-tuning is a technique in which a language model is trained on data
derived from a validated knowledge graph. This approach supports factual grounding and
improves the model's ability to answer questions consistently with the encoded knowledge.

Neuro-symbolic integration supports reasoning, factual grounding, and alignment in small
language models trained on structured domain knowledge.
